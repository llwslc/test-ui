Typography Declares Its Independence

After years trapped in rounded cards, the written word has returned to the front page.

At precisely nine this morning, the grid stopped apologising. Headlines expanded across five columns, rules became visible again, and information remembered that hierarchy can be both useful and beautiful. The Gazette treats typography not as content poured into containers, but as architecture. Hairline rules organise without decoration. Ink blue marks action without shouting. Every hover earns its underline. Every column carries a clear measure and a deliberate rag. This is not nostalgia. It is a working editorial machine, built for subjects with consequences and readers with curiosity.

“The page found its voice when the boxes went quiet.”
