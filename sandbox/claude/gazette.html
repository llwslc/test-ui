From the composing room

The Night Desk Sets a Paper That Changes Shape

A new case of type came up the goods lift at midnight. By the second edition the compositors had run the same front page six ways, and none of them looked borrowed.

By The Night Editor  ·  Filed 04:12 from the Quay Street works

Long after the last tram had gone up the hill, the composing room still smelled of hot metal and turpentine, and the men on the night side were arguing about column measure. The case that arrived at midnight was not catalogued. It carried no maker's plate, only a stencil on the lid reading PROTEUS, and beneath that, in a smaller hand, set as required.

The foreman opened it under the one good lamp. Inside were faces the paper had run for forty years and a dozen it had never owned: a blackletter for the nameplate, a Didone cut fine enough to hurt at fourteen point, a mono for the wire slips. Every sort sat true in its box. Nothing was worn.

“We set the shipping tables in it first,” the foreman said, “because if a thing survives the shipping tables it will survive anything.” The tables came off the stone at ten to two, clean, and the desk sent them up without a query.

What unsettled the night side was not the type but the register. The blue plate hangs a hair off the black on every sheet that comes down the belt — a hair, no more, the width of a good rule — and the pressmen have twice reset the drum without closing it. The ink is dry by the vans. The fringe stays.

The publisher was telephoned at three and declined to stop the run. “It reads,” she said. “Send it out.” Forty thousand copies went to the vans on time, and the crossword, corrected twice, went with them.

The case is to be inventoried this morning by the works manager, who has asked that nobody move it. The night side has already moved it, twice, closer to the stone.

The Composing Room

Every control on this page is a slug of type. Press one and the sheet takes the impression.

Actions at the stone

Ink — the committed action, one to a section Rule — the reversible action Blue — touches the second plate Plain — the quiet action, its rule lit blue on hover Hatched — unavailable until the forme is locked

On the impression

A slug sits proud of the paper until it is struck. Hold any button down and the sheet gives, the shadow inverts, and the type settles a point into the stock — the same debossed edge you can read with a thumbnail on the back of the sheet.

Hover carries the second smell of the room: ink that has not dried. The rule under a quiet action lights blue, and the ink blooms a half-millimetre past its own edge before it sets.

    Quay Street, 03:44 — Gazette staff

    The works manager has asked that the unmarked case of type be left where it stands until it can be entered in the inventory. The night side has moved it twice, both times toward the stone, and neither man will say who moved it second.

    A count taken at four found every sort present and no face duplicated. The foreman notes that the mono in the case matches the wire slips to the tenth of a point, which he calls a coincidence and the desk calls something else.

    The river, 02:15 — Harbour desk

    Fog closed the lower reach at midnight and the pilot boats held. Three coasters lie off the bar waiting for the tide, and the bridge is out at the crossing, which suspends rural delivery for a fourth week.

    The tide tables set on Tuesday stand corrected: high water reads 06:52, not 06:25. Subscribers taking the tables will find the amended slip folded into tomorrow's morning edition.

    Exchange floor, 23:50 — Markets desk

    Paper stock closed firm on the strength of the winter contracts. Ink, both black and reflex blue, held at yesterday's figure; the blue is quoted thin and the works has taken forward cover through March.

    Type metal moved a quarter point on news of a foundry closing upriver. Trade subscribers will find the full table set in the evening final, where it belongs.

    From the roof, 04:00 — Observer

    Fog on the river until nine, lifting before the trucks load. Wind south-west at eight knots, backing southerly by afternoon. Rain holds off past the second edition and arrives with the evening vans.

    The roof gauge has read a quarter inch since Sunday. The observer reminds the desk, again, that he is not responsible for what the vans meet on the hill.

    The fudge box. Anything filed here goes on the front of whatever is still on the drum.

    Hold the front page

    Stopping the run costs eleven minutes and the vans wait. The night editor is told either way.

    Why eleven minutes Four to bring the web down, five to swap the plate, two to bring her back to speed. The vans hold at the dock and the hill run goes late.

    Colophon

    The Proteus Gazette, city edition, printed nightly at the Quay Street works on a two-plate web press: ink black and reflex blue.
    Nameplate set in UnifrakturMaguntia. Headlines in Bodoni Moda. Text in Source Serif 4. Wire slips, folios and rules in IBM Plex Mono.
    Register is checked against the corner targets on every sheet. Tonight it reads open by a hair, and the publisher declines to stop the run.

    Hold the front page?

    The web comes down at the current copy. Eleven minutes to swap the plate and bring her back to speed; the vans hold at the dock and the hill run goes late.

    The night editor is notified either way. Copies already folded go out as printed.
