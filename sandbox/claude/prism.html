01 / METHOD

Three primaries.
Three forms. One rule.

Kandinsky handed each colour a shape. We hand each shape a job. That mapping is the whole grammar of the kit.

Red · Square

Commit

The square sits still and carries weight. So it does the committing — save, send, stamp. The primary action is always red.

Yellow · Triangle

Direct

The triangle points and carries motion. So it does the directing — next, more, go. Wherever the eye should move, a yellow edge leans.

Blue · Circle

Wait

The circle turns and carries time. So it does the waiting — load, sync, hold. Every progress and pause is drawn in blue.

02 / CATALOGUE

Parts, flat
and offset once.

Every control on the sheet is live. Press one and watch it fall into its own shadow — that offset is the only depth we allow.

Part 01 — Actuators

Buttons commit, direct, wait.

Five faces, one press. Red commits, yellow directs, blue waits; black is the neutral, ghost is the quiet. The sixth is spent.

Part 02 — Fields

Name a part.

Type it, stamp it. The label prints straight onto the ticket — nothing embellished.

Flat fill, offset once, set on the module. That is the entire trick — and the entire style guide.
Part 03 — Choices

Squares check. Circles pick.

Checks fill red and square; the radio fills a blue circle; the switch throws yellow. The forms never trade jobs.

Part 06 — Panels

Tabs slide the block.

Selecting a tab throws a solid blue block into place — no dissolve, just a hard move to the next bay.

Bay one — Cut

Trim the sheet to the module. Anything past the grid comes off; the rule stays a single thick black line.

Bay two — Stamp

Lay the primary flat, no blend. One press of red, yellow or blue; the ink never gradients toward the edge.

Bay three — Set

Drop the part on the grid and offset the shadow once. Solid black, nine pixels down and right. Done.

Part 07 — Signals

Confirm, notify, explain.

The dialog slams in with a red offset. Notices deal in from the right and clear themselves. The tip waits under a yellow disc until you ask.

Signals borrow the same three primaries: red confirms a commit, blue files a wait, yellow directs you on.
